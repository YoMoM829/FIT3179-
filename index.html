Energy Production/Consumption
When it comes to Australia, it has a population of 26.01 million. This is quite a small population given the size of Australia. However, Australia is a Developed country and quite technologically advanced compared to most other countries. In fact, Australia is the 20th largest consumer of Energy on the Planet. Additionally, Australia is the 15th largest consumer per capita energy use which refers to the amount of energy used by each Australian.

Coal is the energy source used to produce the most energy for Australia. Whilst Renewables is the least produced. This is because Coal is the most abundant source energy resource in Australia and is often mined the most.

Renewables have only been propped in the last 3 or so decades as more concern has been brought about the environmental impact of energy. Though given the current pressure on Climate Change, in the future, Renewables increase in energy production.

Gas and Oil do not seem to contribute to much to Energy production. This is due to the fact that Coal outputs more energy per KG. It is more energy efficient and it can be safer to excavate and mine in comparison to Gas.

Energy Prices and Energy Usage

The above chart analyses the market prices of electricity for different states over 30 minute intervals in 2024. As can be seen QLD has the greatest variation in prices with Tasmania having the smallest variation.

In the graph, there seems to Negative Prices. Negative prices indicate that the those who have Solar Panels or some sort of renewable energy technology at their house are able to sell the energy they create back to the electricity network. For a lot of people who have this technology, this means there are able to pay very little on Electricity and thus save a lot of money.

The following bar chart looks at how much energy is consumed by different industries. The three largest being Electricity Supply, Manufacturing, and Transport.

These are the three biggest given that Electricity Supply refers to the supply of electricity to homes, which is high due to the vast amount of uses of technology. Transport is high in energy consumption given the number of people who use Public transport to commute to and from work. Manufacturing is high since a lot of machines are used to make products, and these machines require a lot of power.

Interestingly, energy demand increases from 900PJ to 1800PJ, and correspondingly the power demand for these industries, in fact, for all of them, increases as technology becomes more widely used in these sectors.
