Energy Production/Consumption
When it comes to Australia, it has a population of 26.01 million. This is actually quite a small population given the size of Australia. However, Australia is a Developed country and quite technologically advanced comapred to most other countries. In fact, Australia is the 20th largest consume of Energy on the Planet. Additionally, Australia is the 15th in terms of per capita energy use which refers to the amount of energy used by each Australian.
Coal is the energy source used to produce the most energy for Australia. Whilst Renewables is the least produced.

This is because Coal is the most abundant source energy resource in Australia and is often mined the most.

Renewables have only been propped in the last 3 or so decades as more concern has been brought about the environmental impact of energy.
A similar trend is seen in the line chart above where Coal seems to be resource where the energy produced from it is consumed.

However, it is shown that Oil is actually larger in comparison. Ironically, Coal should be the largest contributor to Energy Consumption as the Energy Produced by Coal should be the Energy Consumed.

What does hold True is that Renewables contribute the least since they have only recently been introduced into the Australian Energy Sector.
Imports/Exports of Energy

Due to the large amount of resources, Australia is one of the biggest exporters of resources in the world. It is also a major importer of resources and mainly Imports to reduce costs and improve the efficiency of processes such as transportation and mineral refinement.

It has a plethora of abudnant resources such as LNG, Uranium, Coal and Natural Gas and takes advantageous of these resources by exporting these resouces to countries in need of Energy resources such as South Korea and Japan.
It exports mainly to large countries and priortises prices and demand towards countries such as India and China which makes its a staple of energy provider for these countries.
Australia exports in large bundles due to the excellent infrastructure of its ports and mining facilities. Additionally, to accomdate for the growing need of energy, Australia has priortised improving current facilities for mining and mineral refinement.
